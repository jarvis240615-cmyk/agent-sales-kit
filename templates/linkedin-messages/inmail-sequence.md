# LinkedIn InMail Sequence (3-Touch)

## InMail 1 — Day 1
**Subject:** [Specific to their situation — max 50 chars]

Hi {{first_name}},

[One sentence showing you know their world — reference company news, growth, or role-specific challenge.]

We've helped [similar companies] solve [specific problem] — [one-line result].

Worth a quick 15-min call to see if there's a fit for {{company}}?

{{sender_name}} | {{sender_title}}, {{sender_company}}

---

## InMail 2 — Day 7
**Subject:** Re: [previous subject]

Hi {{first_name}},

Following up — sharing a relevant case study: [2-line summary of win at similar company].

Happy to send the full details if useful.

{{sender_name}}

---

## InMail 3 — Day 14
**Subject:** Closing the loop — {{company}}

Hi {{first_name}},

Last message. If the timing isn't right or you're not the best person for this, totally understand.

Feel free to reach out if [problem area] comes up: {{sender_email}}

{{sender_name}}

---

## Response Handling

**If interested:**
Reply within 2 hours. Offer 2 specific time slots. Send calendar link (Calendly/Google Meet).
*"Great to hear! Are you free [Day] at [Time] or [Day] at [Time] IST? Here's my calendar: [link]"*

**If "not now":**
*"Totally understand. When would be a better time — next quarter or after [event/deadline]? I'll follow up then."*

**If "wrong person":**
*"Thanks for letting me know! Who would be the best person to speak with about [topic] at {{company}}?"*

**If no response after InMail 3:**
Move to email sequence. Wait 5 days before first email.

**If "send me info":**
This is a soft yes. Send:
1. Company one-pager (PDF)
2. 1 relevant case study
3. Calendar link
Follow up in 3 days if no response.

---

## Tips
- Send InMails Tuesday–Thursday, 9–11am local time
- Keep subject lines specific — generic subjects get ignored
- Reference their recent LinkedIn activity or company news in InMail 1
- Don't mention price in any InMail
- Max 150 words per InMail
