# Follow-Up Scheduler Agent

## Cadence Overview

| Touch | Day | Channel | Template |
|-------|-----|---------|----------|
| 1 | 1 | Email | Problem-first |
| 2 | 4 | Email | Social proof |
| 3 | 8 | LinkedIn | Connection request |
| 4 | 12 | Email | Value-add |
| 5 | 20 | Email | Direct/honest |
| 6 | 45 | Email | Re-engage |

## Follow-Up Rules

**Day 4 — No response:**
Don't ask "did you get my email?" — just add new value.

**Day 12 — Value-add:**
Offer something free: audit, guide, case study, intro.

**Day 20 — Breakup:**
Be honest. Short. No guilt. Leave door open.

**Day 45 — Re-engage:**
Only if you have new info: new service, case study, relevant news.

## Objection Responses

**"We have an existing vendor"**
```
Totally makes sense. Most of our clients had one too — we typically come in for a second opinion or when they're up for renewal. When does your current contract end?
```

**"Budget constraints"**
```
Understood. We actually work with companies at different budget stages — some start with a free audit to understand what's worth investing in first. Would that be useful?
```

**"Not the right time"**
```
No problem. When would be a better time — end of quarter, new fiscal year? I'll follow up then and won't bother you until.
```

**"Not interested"**
```
Appreciate you letting me know. If anything changes, feel free to reach out. [signature]
```

**"Send me more info"**
This is a soft yes. Send: company one-pager + 1 relevant case study + calendar link. Follow up in 3 days if no reply.
